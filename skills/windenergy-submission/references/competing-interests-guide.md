# Competing Interests Declaration Guide

Use this guide for target journals that require a Declaration of Competing
Interests or Declaration of Interest.

## Requirements

- All authors **must disclose** any financial and personal relationships that could inappropriately influence or bias their work
- The declaration is submitted as a **separate .doc/.docx file** uploaded at the "attach/upload files" step
- Author signatures are **not required**
- The declarations tool in the submission system should always be completed

## Examples of competing interests

- Employment relationships
- Consultancies
- Stock ownership
- Honoraria (paid speaking or writing)
- Paid expert testimony
- Patent applications or registrations
- Grants or any other funding
- Affiliation with the journal as Editor or Advisory Board Member

## When no competing interests exist

If all authors have nothing to declare, select "I have nothing to declare" in the submission system.

The uploaded .docx should contain a clear statement:

```text
Declaration of Competing Interests

The authors declare that they have no known competing financial interests
or personal relationships that could have appeared to influence the work
reported in this paper.
```

## When competing interests exist

Each interest should be described clearly:

```text
Declaration of Competing Interests

Author A reports financial support from [Company] for the research
described in this paper. Author B serves on the advisory board of
[Organization], which is related to the subject matter of this paper.
Authors C, D, and E declare no competing interests.
```

## Journal editor affiliation

If any author has a journal affiliation (Editor, Advisory Board), they must include this text under "Other Activities" in the declarations tool:

```text
Given their role as [insert journal role title], [insert your name] had
no involvement in the peer-review of this article and has no access to
information regarding its peer-review. Full responsibility for the
editorial process for this article was delegated to another journal editor.
```

The author must also inform the journal and publisher prior to completing submission.

## Writing process

### Step 1. Ask each author

For every author on the manuscript, ask:
1. Do you have any financial relationships relevant to this work?
2. Do you have personal relationships that could bias this work?
3. Do you have any employment, consultancy, or patent interests related to this topic?
4. Do you have a journal affiliation (Editor, Advisory Board)?

### Step 2. Draft the declaration

- If no interests: use the standard "nothing to declare" statement
- If interests exist: describe each one clearly, naming the author and the specific interest

### Step 3. Save and prepare for upload

- Save as .doc or .docx format
- No signatures required
- Upload at the "attach/upload files" step in the submission system
- Also complete the declarations tool in the online submission system

## Common issues for Chinese authors

### Issue 1: University holds a patent

If the institution holds a patent related to the work, this should be disclosed as a competing interest for the relevant authors.

### Issue 2: Wind farm operator provided data

Receiving data under NDA is usually not a competing interest, but the relationship should be disclosed in the Data Availability statement. If the operator also funded the research, that is a competing interest.

### Issue 3: Nothing to declare

This is perfectly acceptable and common. Use the standard statement template.

## QA checklist

- [ ] All authors considered
- [ ] Declaration saved as .doc/.docx
- [ ] File ready to upload at submission
- [ ] Online declarations tool completed
- [ ] Journal editor affiliations disclosed (if applicable)
- [ ] No signatures required (confirmed not included)
