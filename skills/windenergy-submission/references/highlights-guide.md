# Highlights Guide

Use this guide for all target journals. Check `journal-profiles.md` to see
whether highlights are required or encouraged.

## Common Format

- 3 to 5 bullet points.
- 85 characters or fewer per bullet, including spaces.
- Editable text file when submitted separately.
- No references, citations, equations, unsupported numbers, or generic claims.

## Content Order

1. Novel method, dataset, benchmark, or engineering question.
2. Main quantitative result.
3. Robustness, ablation, cross-site generalization, or physical insight.
4. Practical implication for energy operation, forecasting, integration, or
   decision support.

## Wind And AI Patterns

```text
- Wake-aware graph model improves wind power forecasting during ramp events
- Chronological tests avoid leakage across two years of SCADA records
- Cross-farm validation shows robust gains under seasonal wind variability
```

## Compression Rules

- Replace long phrases with direct nouns and verbs.
- Keep one claim per bullet.
- Use standard abbreviations only when widely recognized or already defined.
- Keep quantitative claims exactly aligned with supplied results.

## QA

- Count every character including spaces.
- Verify that each bullet is supported by the manuscript.
- Verify that at least one bullet gives an energy-system implication.
- Mark missing quantitative evidence as `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED`.
