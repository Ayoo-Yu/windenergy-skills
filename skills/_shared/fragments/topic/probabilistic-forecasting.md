# Topic Fragment: Probabilistic Forecasting

## Activation Condition

Load when the manuscript predicts distributions, quantiles, intervals, risk
levels, uncertainty, or reliability.

## Scope

Use for uncertainty quality, reliability, sharpness, calibration, scoring rules,
and decision value.

## Allowed Content

Reliability, sharpness, interval or quantile evaluation, scoring rules,
uncertainty visualization, decision cost, and risk-aware interpretation.

## Forbidden Content

Specific conformal algorithms or named statistical tests unless the conformal
calibration fragment is active.

## Output Influence

Explain why uncertainty quality matters for the energy decision, then report
both reliability and informativeness.

## Audit Influence

Audits may check that uncertainty claims include both reliability and
informativeness when the manuscript makes both kinds of claim.

## Examples

"A forecast interval claim should state what reliability and sharpness mean in
the application."

## Anti Examples

"Every probabilistic paper must run one named backtest or use one named score."
