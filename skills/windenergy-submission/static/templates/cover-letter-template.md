# Cover Letter Template

```text
[Date]

Dear Editor,

Subject: Submission of "[Article Title]" as a [Article Type]
    in [Target Journal]

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "[Article Title]" for
consideration as a [Article Type] in [Target Journal].

[Paragraph 2: Problem and findings: 3-4 sentences]
[The specific renewable energy problem addressed.] [The main approach or
method proposed.] [The principal finding(s), with quantitative results.]
[Why this matters for renewable energy.]

[Paragraph 3: Scope fit: 2-3 sentences]
This work addresses [Target Journal]'s scope by [specific contribution].
[Broader implication for the active energy domain, system operation, planning,
market decision, AI methodology, or pattern recognition, as appropriate.]
[Optional: Contribution to SDG 7 / SDG 13.]

All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to
[Target Journal].

[If invited submission: We are grateful for the invitation to submit this
work to [Target Journal].]

We look forward to your consideration.

Sincerely,

[Corresponding Author Full Name]
[Title, e.g., Professor / PhD Candidate]
[Department]
[Institution]
[Full postal address]
[Email]
[Phone]
```

## Paragraph 2 fill-in patterns

### Application method paper

```text
[Application task] remains challenging because [field tension or operational
constraint]. We propose [method name] that integrates [domain knowledge] into
[architecture or workflow]. The method achieves [evidence summary] compared to
[baseline] across [study units], with particularly strong performance under
[condition].
```

### Optimization or planning paper

```text
Optimizing [energy asset or system decision] while managing [constraint] is a
complex decision problem. We develop [method] that [approach]. The framework
improves [objective] by [X] compared to [baseline], validated using [data,
simulation, or case setting].
```

### Condition monitoring

```text
Early detection of [asset or system anomaly] can reduce operational risk. We
propose [method] that [approach] using [data source] from [study setting]. The
method achieves [metric] with [risk or false-alarm measure], outperforming
[baseline approaches].
```

## Forbidden content reminder

Do NOT include in the cover letter:
- Funding information or grant numbers
- Author declarations (competing interests, ethics)
- Suggested or opposed reviewers
- Content that belongs in the manuscript itself

## Length check

- Target: 300-400 words (under one page at 11-12pt)
- Maximum: follow the target journal profile
