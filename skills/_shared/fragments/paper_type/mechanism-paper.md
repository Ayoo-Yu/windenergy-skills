# Paper Type Fragment: Mechanism Paper

## Activation Condition

Load when the paper explains why a phenomenon or method behavior occurs.

## Scope

Use for mechanism explanation, operating boundaries, rival explanations, and
evidence strength.

## Allowed Content

Hypothesis framing, mechanism signatures, falsification logic, boundary
conditions, rival explanations, and cautious causal language.

## Forbidden Content

Single-paper numbers, topic-specific tests, model lists, dataset names, or
journal thresholds.

## Output Influence

Introduction should foreground the unexplained phenomenon. Discussion should
separate observation, mechanism interpretation, and remaining alternatives.

## Audit Influence

Strong mechanism verbs require direct controls or a clear downgrade to
empirical signature language.

## Results-Discussion Interaction Rules

Mechanism papers require strict separation between observation and
interpretation because the central claim is a causal or mechanistic
explanation.

### Observation vs Interpretation Separation

| Layer | Content | Section |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Observation | Measured pattern, effect size, statistical test result | Results |
| Bridge | One sentence connecting observation to next analysis | Results |
| Mechanism interpretation | Why the pattern occurred, which mechanism explains it | Discussion |
| Rival alternatives | Other mechanisms that could explain the same pattern | Discussion |
| Boundary | Conditions where the mechanism explanation stops holding | Discussion |
| Synthesis | Final mechanistic judgment based on evidence weight | Conclusion |

### Rules

- Results must report the pattern before Discussion names the mechanism.
  Do not present the mechanism as established in Results.
- If a Results paragraph contains "because", "due to", or "reflects",
  move the causal claim to Discussion.
- Discussion must state at least one rival explanation for the central
  finding before endorsing the preferred mechanism.
- When evidence is ambiguous between mechanisms, Discussion must
  preserve the ambiguity rather than resolve it by assertion.
- The mechanism explanation in Discussion should be layered:
  1. Statistical mechanism: what the diagnostics reveal
  2. Evaluation implication: what this means for measurement practice
  3. Operational implication: what this means for deployment (qualified)
- Conclusion should synthesize which mechanism the evidence weight
  supports, not re-report the observations.

## Examples

"The results identify an operating boundary where the method changes its
behavior."

"The observed sensitivity to [parameter] is consistent with [mechanism A]
but does not rule out [mechanism B], which would require [stated
experiment] to isolate."

## Anti Examples

"This fragment requires a named statistical test or a named benchmark count."

"The mechanism is clearly established by the results." (Too strong without
rival explanation and controls.)
