# Cover Letter Guide

Use this guide with `journal-profiles.md`.

## When A Cover Letter Is Required

The local profiles mark a cover letter as required for:

- `renewable-energy`: max 1 page.
- `energy-conversion-management`: max 1 page.
- `pattern-recognition`: required and must answer journal-specific editor
  questions.

For other journals, a short cover letter can still be useful when the
submission system allows it.

## Intake

Collect:

- Target journal slug.
- Article title and article type.
- Corresponding author name, institution, and email.
- Main research question.
- Main method.
- Main quantitative findings.
- Scope-fit reason for the target journal.
- Confirmation that all authors approve submission.
- Confirmation that the manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere.

## Structure

1. Submission introduction: title, article type, and target journal.
2. Problem and findings: one short paragraph with the method and main evidence.
3. Scope fit: explain why the journal readership should care.
4. Closing: author approval, exclusivity, and corresponding-author contact.

## Target Notes

- Renewable Energy: emphasize renewable energy systems, wind energy technology,
  power generation, and scope fit. Avoid funding information, declarations, and
  reviewer suggestions in the letter.
- Energy Conversion and Management: emphasize conversion, management, system
  performance, and practical energy value.
- Pattern Recognition: answer the journal-specific editor questions before or
  within the letter. Ask the user for those questions if they are not supplied.

## Red Lines

- Do not fabricate corresponding-author details.
- Do not include grants, conflicts, or data declarations unless the target
  guide explicitly asks for them in the cover letter.
- Do not suggest or oppose reviewers unless the target system requests that
  information and the user supplies names.
- Do not overstate novelty or claim journal fit without manuscript evidence.

## Compact Template

```text
Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled "[TITLE]" for consideration as a [ARTICLE
TYPE] in [JOURNAL].

[Two to four sentences stating the problem, method, dataset or system, and main
verified findings.]

We believe the manuscript fits [JOURNAL] because [target-specific scope reason].
For wind and AI manuscripts, state how the method supports renewable generation,
forecasting, grid integration, operation, control, or decision support.

All authors have approved this submission, and the manuscript is not under
consideration elsewhere.

Sincerely,
[Corresponding Author]
```
