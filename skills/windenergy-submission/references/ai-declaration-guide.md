# Generative AI Declaration Guide

Use this guide for all 18 target journals. Elsevier journals require authors to
assess and disclose generative AI or AI-assisted technology use during
manuscript preparation when such tools were used.

## Required Intake

Ask the author for:

1. Tool or service name.
2. Provider and version when known.
3. Purpose of use: language editing, literature organization, code assistance,
   data analysis, figure generation, or another task.
4. Whether any AI-generated content remains in the manuscript.
5. Whether AI was used to create or alter artwork.

## When A Declaration Is Needed

Prepare a declaration when the author used tools such as ChatGPT, Claude,
Gemini, Copilot, AI agents, or deep research tools for manuscript preparation,
language improvement beyond basic grammar checking, literature organization,
data-analysis assistance, code assistance that affected manuscript content, or
content organization.

## Usually No Declaration Needed

Basic spell checking, simple grammar checking, reference managers, and standard
search engines usually do not require a generative AI declaration. Still report
the basis for that decision if the user asks.

## Standard Template

```text
Declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the manuscript
preparation process

During the preparation of this work the author(s) used [NAME OF TOOL / SERVICE]
in order to [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the author(s) reviewed and
edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of
the published article.
```

## No AI Use

Do not add an empty declaration section unless the target journal system asks
for one. If the author reports no generative AI use, state that no declaration
is drafted based on the provided information.

## Artwork Rule

Flag any AI-created or AI-altered artwork for journal-specific review. Elsevier
energy journals commonly restrict generative AI use in artwork unless AI use is
part of the research design and is documented in Methods.

## Red Lines

- Do not list AI tools as authors.
- Do not create a final declaration until the author provides actual tool use.
- Do not hide AI use that materially affected manuscript preparation.
- Do not claim compliance if figures, graphical abstracts, or visual assets may
  have been AI-generated without disclosure.
